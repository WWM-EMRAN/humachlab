About

This is EMRAN. I want to enjoy each moment of my life. I better like to be simple and like simplicity and nature. I am a friendly and travel maniac.
That was my personal life, in my professional life I am very much enthusiastic about learning new things, especially those that I like. Life science was always intriguing to me. As an IT professional, I want to utilize my IT knowledge in the field of medical science. Biomedical science and Machine Learning allow me to achieve this dream.
I studied bachelors degree for 4 years in Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) at Hajee Mohammad Danesh Science and Technology University (HSTU), Bangladesh, and ranked first in my batch. During that time I was involved in various voluntary activities. I was also involved in different activities including tech event management and participation in computer programming contests.
After my graduation, I worked in software development industries in my home country for almost 3 years and developed many iOS apps individually as well as in a group. Within this period, I had been promoted to senior software engineer and software development manager while achieving the best employee award for the recognition of my devotion and hard work.
Later, I worked as an academician and faculty member under the department of CSE at HSTU for more than 5 years. I conducted different major courses, also involved in research works and research supervision.
I achieved the Bangabandhu Science and Technology Fellowship (BSTF) funded by the Ministry of Science and Technology, the People’s Republic of Bangladesh for my higher studies. Then I finished my 2-year Master of Science degree by research in Information Technology (IT) from Deakin University, Australia. My research is mainly based on signal processing, machine learning, and optimization. To be specific Brain signal (Electroencephalograms-EEG) analysis and apply machine (deep) learning method for disease/disorder detection or prediction. In this two-year period, I have been able to publish two conference and journal papers along with a feature extractor library in Python language which is almost ready to be published for the research community.
I have also worked as a research assistant in a project at Deakin University in collaboration with Monash University and Alfred Health for detecting/predicting epileptic seizures in ICU patients. Another project in colaboration with Monash University and AETMOS, I worked on indoor air temperature prediction for air quality forcasting.
I have been selected as a Cotutelle scholar to complete my Doctor of Phylosophy (PhD) degree from Deaking University, Australia in colaboration with Coventry University, United Kingdom.
I will be doing my research on sleep and aging.
I have good management, leadership, and technical skills with mastering skills in programming which I achieved from the practices and experiences from previous academic and professional institutions. I am looking forward to utilizing my research knowledge and development skills to come up with industry-grade (mobile) products.
